# Transforming Retail: 99% Inventory Accuracy with RFID & Digital Twins 🚀

**Retail Business Management | Sector: Supermarkets & Pharmacies**

---

## 1. Executive Summary
This project addresses one of the most critical challenges in modern retail: **Out-of-Stock (OOS)** events and operational inefficiency in inventory management. In high-turnover sectors such as supermarkets and pharmacies, traditional inventory accuracy fluctuates between 65-75%.

This proposal implements an **RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)** model integrated with a **Digital Twin** of the store. The objective is to achieve **99% accuracy**, reduce shrinkage by 20%, and enable error-free **omnichannel logistics** (Click & Collect).

---

## 2. Problem Definition
Current retail operations suffer from a **"visibility gap"**—a discrepancy between system records and physical shelf availability.

* **Impact on Supermarkets:** Revenue loss due to "unfound" products and high waste of perishables.
* **Impact on Pharmacies:** Risk of expired medications and loss of traceability for critical health products.
* **Root Cause:** Manual counting processes, human error during receiving, and lack of real-time data integration.

---

## 3. Proposed Solution: Project Architecture

### A. Technological Components
* **Source Tagging:** Implementation of RFID tags at the supplier level for end-to-end traceability.
* **Sensors & Infrastructure:** Fixed readers at strategic points (receiving docks, sales floor, exits) and handheld PDAs for rapid cyclic counting.
* **Digital Twin:** A virtual replica of the store powered by real-time sensor data, allowing managers to visualize the physical location of every item.

### B. Operational Value Flow
1.  **Automated Receiving:** Pallets pass through an RFID portal; the system validates 100% of the content against the Purchase Order in seconds without opening boxes.
2.  **Intelligent Replenishment:** The system detects when an item leaves the sales area (via POS sale) and triggers an automated alert to the replenisher if floor stock reaches the reorder point.
3.  **Loss Prevention:** Immediate alerts if a product exits the store without being processed through the POS.

## 4. Business Analysis

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| Indicator | Before (Manual/Barcode) | After (RFID + Digital Twin) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inventory Accuracy** | 70% | **99%** |
| **Store Counting Time** | 8 - 12 Hours | **15 - 30 Minutes** |
| **Lost Sales (OOS)** | 8% | **< 2%** |
| **Shrinkage Rate (Expiry)** | High (Difficult to control) | **Low (Preventive Alerts)** |

### ROI Estimation (Return on Investment)
The project reaches its **break-even point within 14 to 18 months** through:
* Drastic reduction in man-hours for physical inventories.
* Recovery of lost sales due to "phantom stock" visibility.
* Working capital optimization by eliminating "just-in-case" overstocking.

---

## 5. Implementation Roadmap

* **Phase 1: Diagnosis & Pilot (Months 1-3):** Selection of a model store and categorization of high-value/high-rotation products (e.g., dermocosmetics in pharmacies).
* **Phase 2: Systems Integration (Months 4-6):** Connecting RFID middleware with the ERP (**SAP/Oracle**) and developing the **Power BI** control dashboard.
* **Phase 3: Scalability (Months 7-12):** Full chain rollout and operational staff training.
